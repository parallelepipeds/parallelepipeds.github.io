Rui-Jie Yew (she/they)

About

I'm a rising senior at Scripps College pursuing a computer science and mathematics major through Harvey Mudd College.

My current research interests lie in the embedding of human values and policy–especially pertaining to AI ethics and privacy–into technical systems.

This fall, I’m on a gap semester doing data science work at the U.S. Census Bureau by way of a Civic Digital Fellowship.

Last summer, I was part of the inaugural cohort of privacy engineer interns at Google, where I participated in privacy reviews for home devices and wrote code to achieve (ε,0)-differential privacy for Privacy on Beam when partitions are specified beforehand and Laplace noise is used.

I'm also fortunate to have been advised by Pavel Naumov, George Montañez, and Xanda Schofield on research and independent study projects.
